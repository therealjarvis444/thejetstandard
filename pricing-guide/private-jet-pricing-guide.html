Example: A light jet that normally costs $6,000/hour may be available for $2,500-$3,000/hour on an empty leg route.

The Hidden Costs of Commercial First-Class

When calculating the true cost of commercial travel, consider these often-overlooked expenses:

  • Hotel nights: Overnight stays due to connections or schedule constraints ($300-$800/night)
  • Rental cars/ground transport: Airport transfers and local transportation ($100-$400/trip)
  • Meals: Airport dining and in-flight purchases beyond included meals ($50-$200)
  • Time lost: For business travelers, 4-6 hours of lost productivity per trip (value varies by professional)
  • Connection risks: Missed meetings due to delays, cancellations, or baggage issues
  • Ground time: Extended layovers that add hours to journey time

3. Empty Leg Deals Explained

Empty leg flights represent one of the best values in private aviation. Understanding how they work can unlock significant savings for flexible travelers.

What Are Empty Leg Flights?

When a private jet is chartered for a one-way trip, it often needs to return to its home base or reposition to pick up the next client. These repositioning flights—called "empty legs," "deadhead flights," or "ferry flights"—would otherwise fly without revenue-generating passengers.

Operators prefer to sell these seats at discounted rates rather than fly empty, creating opportunities for cost-conscious travelers.

How to Access Empty Leg Deals

  1. Join deal alert lists: Subscribe to empty leg notifications from charter providers
  2. Be flexible with dates: Empty legs are date-specific; flexibility increases options
  3. Consider nearby airports: Expanding your departure/arrival radius reveals more deals
  4. Book with short notice: Many empty legs are posted 24-72 hours before departure
  5. Work with a broker: Experienced brokers track empty leg inventory across multiple operators

4. Booking Process

Chartering a private jet is straightforward when you understand the process. Here's what to expect from initial inquiry to wheels-up.

  • Submit Your Trip Request Provide departure/arrival airports, preferred dates/times, passenger count, and any special requirements (pets, catering, ground transport).
  • Receive Aircraft Options & Quotes Within 1-4 hours, you'll receive tailored options with aircraft details, pricing breakdown, and availability. Multiple aircraft categories may be presented for comparison.
  • Select Aircraft & Review Contract Choose your preferred option and review the charter agreement. This includes terms, cancellation policy, and what's included in your quote.
  • Secure Booking with Deposit Most operators require a deposit (typically 25-50%) to secure the aircraft. Payment is processed via wire transfer, credit card, or approved payment method.
  • Provide Passenger Details Submit passenger names, weights (for weight & balance), special requests, and catering preferences. This is typically due 24-48 hours before departure.
  • Receive Final Confirmation 12-24 hours before departure, you'll receive final details including tail number, FBO location, pilot contact, and exact departure time.
  • Arrive at FBO & Depart Arrive 15-20 minutes before departure at the Fixed Base Operator (FBO) terminal. Enjoy private lounge amenities before boarding your aircraft.

Timeline: How Far in Advance to Book

  • Peak times (holidays, major events): 2-4 weeks recommended
  • Standard travel periods: 3-7 days typically sufficient
  • Last-minute availability: Often possible within 24 hours (subject to aircraft availability)
  • Empty leg deals: Usually 24-72 hours before departure

What's Included in Your Charter Price

  • Aircraft and crew (pilots, flight attendants on larger aircraft)
  • Fuel and standard insurance
  • Landing fees and handling charges
  • Standard catering (refreshments, light meals)
  • Ground transportation coordination
  • Flight planning and weather monitoring
  • 24/7 operational support

Note: Premium catering, specific ground transport, de-icing, and international fees may be additional.

5. Frequently Asked Questions

What safety certifications should I look for?

Reputable operators maintain ARG/US Platinum or Gold ratings, Wyvern Wingman certification, and/or IS-BAO Stage II or III registration. All charter flights operate under FAA Part 135 regulations, which require rigorous maintenance schedules, pilot training, and operational oversight. Always verify your operator's safety credentials before booking.

Can I bring pets on private flights?

Yes—private aviation is exceptionally pet-friendly. Most operators welcome pets in the cabin with you. Some may require advance notice, health certificates, or pet carriers. Certain operators provide pet amenities (water bowls, blankets, treats). Always disclose pets when booking to ensure appropriate aircraft preparation.

What is the cancellation policy?

Cancellation terms vary by operator and fare type. Standard charters typically allow free cancellation 48-72 hours before departure, with partial charges for later cancellations. Empty leg deals often have stricter policies (non-refundable or limited refund windows). Jet cards and memberships have their own terms. Always review cancellation policies before booking.

What payment methods are accepted?

Most operators accept wire transfers, major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express), and sometimes corporate checks. Wire transfers are preferred for large transactions and may expedite booking confirmation. Credit cards offer convenience and potential rewards but may have transaction limits. Payment is typically required before departure.

Are there weight or baggage restrictions?

Private jets have more generous baggage allowances than commercial airlines, but weight and balance calculations are critical for flight safety. Typical allowances range from 50-100 lbs per passenger depending on aircraft size. Oversized items (golf clubs, skis, musical instruments) are usually accommodated with advance notice. Share your baggage needs during booking.

Can I bring alcohol on board?

Yes, you may bring your preferred beverages, though most operators provide complimentary standard bar service. Premium or specific brand requests can be arranged in advance. All passengers must be of legal drinking age. The pilot in command has final authority regarding alcohol consumption on board.

What happens if weather delays my flight?

Safety is always the priority. If weather makes travel unsafe, your flight will be delayed or rescheduled. Private aviation offers more flexibility than commercial airlines—you can often depart from alternative nearby airports or adjust timing to avoid weather systems. Your operator will proactively communicate options and work to minimize disruption.
